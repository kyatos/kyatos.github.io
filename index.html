bio



I'm kyatos / Ang Kia Yee (she/her), a Singaporean transdisciplinary poet, performance-maker, and slow dream machine who believes in our capacity for compassion, rupture, agency, and change.

I devise speculative, mutative fictions which offer tethers to alternative worlds, bodily states, systems, and relationships. I see such work as forms of adaptive radiation and oracular intervention.

My work has spanned poetry, essays, short stories, plays, performances, audio walks, workshops, and video. It also encompasses counselling work and astrology readings.

In art and writing, I like to make work that is mysterious, atmospheric, temporally-layered, and unknowing. Playing across disciplines, forms, and media, I operate using the logic of poetry — what Carl Phillips describes as patterned language and the meaningful interruption of those patterns.

The fullness of life asks us to be here and now, and this is where I situate myself and my practice.

cv artistic orientation

I was a resident artist for the 2025 AiR Programme at The Godown Arts Centre, Kuala Lumpur and the 2024 Residency at the Intercultural Theatre Institute; a participating artist for Natasha, Singapore Biennale 2022; and a resident writer for Centre 42’s New Scripts Residency 2021.

My writing has appeared in Stand Magazine, Quarterly Literary Review Singapore, SO-FAR, Plural Art Mag, and for exhibitions at Objectifs and Singapore Art Museum.

As a performance-maker, I co-/directed Descendants (2024-ongoing), an interdisciplinary reimagination of Descendants of the Eunuch Admiral by Kuo Pao Kun; Triumph of Our Tired Eyes (2018), an adaptation of Chekhov's Uncle Vanya; and Flicker (2015), a self-written script on loneliness and connection.

I co-/created and performed in sentry (2018) as part of Festival of Audacity in Birmingham, UK; Saint Somebody (2019) at Camden People's Theatre, UK; Loving Things (2020-2021) as a performance video and live excerpt at the Esplanade Concourse, Singapore; and FUME (2022) by Pat Toh at 72-13, Singapore.

I recently founded a theatre collective called zone (境). I am the Co-Founder & was the Company Lead (2020-2024) of Feelers, a research lab of artists and designers focused on the intersections of art and technology. From 2015-2017, I co-founded and co-led theatre collective make/space.

collectives

If every gathering is an opportunity for alternative relations and world-building to take place, a collective is a commitment to a shared set of relations and worlding, which can become a sustained intervention upon the wider ecosystems it is nested within.

Persisting as any collective is the hardest and most important work we can do together.




zone 境

[ website ]

Founder & Artistic Director

A cross-disciplinary theatre company founded in 2025 which gathers at the borders where language fails, to do the impossible work of expressing the unspeakable and unnameable. By conjuring transient zones where alternative realities and ways of being are possible, we remind ourselves of the possibilities of the impossible.

We make theatre that is preoccupied with the symbolic, psychic, and unconscious. Our creative process is intuition-led, and moves beyond the logics of siloed disciplines and traditional narrative structures. We are invested in performances that reverberate past the level of plot—conveying moods, atmospheres, hunches, suspicions, and desires.





Feelers

[ website ]

Co-Founder & Company Lead (2020-2024)

A research lab of artists and designers focused on the intersections of art and technology.

Initiated Feelers in 2020 alone, desiring a space to critically and humanely engage with technology, as well as organizational practices that would allow artists to work full-time while developing their artistic practices with nourishing rhythms.

Built core team of five artists and designers; developed company ethos; established criticality, play, and interdisciplinarity as central approaches. Experimented with looser, more flexible scheduling structures to allow for artist-workers to effectively continue their individual creative practices.

Notable initiatives:

  • How to Start, How to Go On (publication)
  • Writing Machine (workshop and publication)
  • Generative Dreaming (podcast series)
  • I_S_L_A_N_D_S Residency
  • School of Alternate Internets (education)
  • perfor.ml (ArtxTech Lab)

"While art and technology form key fields of research, they continually lead us back to one another. It is with one another that our real work takes place."

astrology

I am a novice astrologer developing my astrological ethos and skills. My practice is influenced by a loose diet of the work of Chris Brennan, Austin Coppock, Melanie Reinhart, Alice Sparkly Kat, Cam White, and SJ Anderson.

I used to offer natal chart readings but am currently on a break.


ethos

My astrological ethos borrows from the general ethos of counselling, which focuses on aiding you in your process of understanding and making meaning of your life on your terms. I serve as a mirror and temporary companion on your journey, without deciding for you what it means and what you must do.

I consider astrological weather as a predictive set of probable energies, but not as diagnostic or directive indicators which undercut your agency as a living conscious being. Rather, they signal incoming energies - challenging, neutral, smooth-flowing - and possible pathways for working with them across individual and collective patterns.

An astrological reading is not coaching or therapy, but a collaborative intervention where I act as your astrological interpreter and facilitator. We think and feel aloud, in conversation, as to what feels resonant/ difficult/ luminous/ important for you as we speak.
