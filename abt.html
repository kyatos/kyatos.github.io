In this realm, I am based in Singapore, where I live and make/ do transdisciplinary work which spans art, writing, and organizing. My work often speaks and moves within the spaces of desire, speculation, futurity and ecology, with undercurrents of love and deep time.

My poetry, short stories, essays and reviews have been published in Quarterly Literary Review Singapore, Mackerel, Plural Art Mag, Stand Magazine, Cha: An Asian Literary Journal, and so-far; as well as for exhibitions such as REFUSE (Singapore Art Museum), immaterial bodies (Objectifs) and Networked Bodies (Supernormal). In 2020, I published a book of poems titled slow dream machine.

More recently, I was a New Scripts resident playwright at Centre 42 (2021); performed in Pat Toh's FUME (2022); and conceptualised + co-organised the 24-Hour Playwriting Competition 2022 under T:>Works. I am a participating artist for Natasha, the Singapore Biennale 2022.

*

I live and make things to demonstrate that other worlds and ways of being are possible, and that we are more than capable of love, regeneration, renewal and healing. To this process, I play with and trouble the bounds of categories and disciplines. I do my best to resist illusory urgency, distraction and noise, in favour of ease and (re)generative attention.

I'm currently mulling over how I can do healing work in the world. I also lead Feelers, an artist-run art & tech label within Potato Productions. I'm sometimes engaged as an editor, arts writer, organizer and producer.

*

Dear friend, I shall love. I hope you will too.
