about






“Attention is the beginning of devotion.” (Mary Oliver)


Hello, I’m Kia Yee or kyatos (she/they), a Singaporean writer and artist who works with text and performance to propose and enact alternative presents and futures.

I use speculation, inquiry, and world-building to dwell on our capacity for rupture and repair. Presently, I’m focused on writing poetry.

I like to make work that is mysterious, beautiful, temporally-layered, and unknowing.

Most of my projects are transdisciplinary — playing across disciplines, forms, and media. My work has spanned poetry, essays, short stories, plays, performances, audio walks, workshops, and video.

✶

My writing practice is varied, but anchored in poetry — what Carl Phillips describes as patterned language and the meaningful interruption of those patterns.

My work has appeared in Quarterly Literary Review Singapore, Stand Magazine, Cha: An Asian Literary Journal, SO-FAR, and Plural Art Mag. I've also written for exhibitions, including REFUSE (Singapore Art Museum), immaterial bodies (Objectifs) and Networked Bodies (Supernormal).

In 2020, I self-published a poetry chapbook titled slow dream machine (2020).

✶

As for my performance work, I consider all of it theatre. I've been trained (to varying degrees!) in ballet, Suzuki Method of Actor Training, Viewpoints, and Odissi. I simultaneously enjoy the freedom that emerges in discipline, and am fascinated by the choreographic potential of unrestrained bodies.

Recent projects include live performances FUME (2022) and Loving Things (to say) (2021); performance video Loving Things (2020); and audio walk 氵pasang (2022).

✶

I was a participating artist for Natasha, Singapore Biennale 2022, a selected participant for da:ns LAB 2023: IDIOSYNCRASIES at Esplanade – Theatres on the Bay, and a resident writer for Centre 42’s New Scripts Residency 2021.

From 2020-2024, I was the Co-Founder & Company Lead of Feelers, a research lab of artists and designers focused on the intersections of art and technology.

I have a BA(Hons) in English Literature and Creative Writing from the University of Warwick.
