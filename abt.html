bio




“Attention is the beginning of devotion.” (Mary Oliver)

Hi, I’m kyatos / Ang Kia Yee (she/her), a Singaporean artist, poet, novice astrologer and trainee counsellor.

Our present crises demand immense collective effort to change our future trajectories. Yet many of us do not have needed access to and practice with our collective capacity to sense, understand, and take action together to realize a different world. We are limited by notions of lack, competition, destruction, fear, and separation taught to us by adverse childhood experiences, education systems, economies of attention, and global corporations who benefit from our negative emotions, addictions, isolation, and mutual rejection of each other.

I seek to world-build from the inside out by expanding our capacities to live full, realized lives that are resilient, flexible, and connected with the cosmos at large. My work seeds itself in healing, dreaming, re-visioning, re-narrating, and re-prophesying the world through encounters with the numinous, speculative, shadowy, spiritual, transcendent, and plural.

To these ends, I practice writing, performance-making, astrology, counselling, and intentional self-unfolding through my own healing, study, embodiment, and life experiences. In particular, I help hold open shared doors to nonlinear and nonpathological frames of viewing and narrating the self, and seek to illuminate the vitality & agency of nonhuman, inanimate, and/ or nonliving things.

I aim to move intuitively and strategically to expand our shared vision of possible worlds and lives; proliferate mystery and beauty; and create spaces for self-inquiry, change, and release.

In art and writing, I like to make work that is mysterious, atmospheric, temporally-layered, and unknowing. Playing across disciplines, forms, and media, my projects are anchored in the logic of poetry — what Carl Phillips describes as patterned language and the meaningful interruption of those patterns.

My work has spanned poetry, essays, short stories, plays, performances, audio walks, workshops, and video.

I was a resident artist for the 2024 Residency at the Intercultural Theatre Institute; a participating artist for Natasha, Singapore Biennale 2022; and a resident writer for Centre 42’s New Scripts Residency 2021.

From 2020-2024, I was the Co-Founder & Company Lead of Feelers, a research lab of artists and designers focused on the intersections of art and technology.
