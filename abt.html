about






“Attention is the beginning of devotion.” (Mary Oliver)


ANG Kia Yee, or kyatos, is a Singaporean writer and artist who proposes and enacts alternative presents and futures through texts and performances. Through speculation, inquiry, and world-building, her work dwells on our capacity for rupture and repair. Presently, she is focused on writing poetry.

Her projects are largely transdisciplinary — and attempt the destabilisation, contamination, mutation, and cross-pollination of disciplines, forms, and media. Her work has spanned poetry, essays, short stories, plays, performances, audio walks, workshops, and video.

✶

Her first poetry chapbook is slow dream machine (2020). Her writing has appeared in Quarterly Literary Review Singapore, Stand Magazine, Cha: An Asian Literary Journal, SO-FAR, and Plural Art Mag. She has also written for exhibitions, including REFUSE (Singapore Art Museum), immaterial bodies (Objectifs) and Networked Bodies (Supernormal).

kyatos’ performance practice is grounded in theatre. Her training experience includes ballet, Suzuki Method of Actor Training, Viewpoints, and Odissi. Recent projects include live performances FUME (2022) and Loving Things (to say) (2021); performance video Loving Things (2020); and audio walk 氵pasang (2022).

She was a participating artist for Natasha, Singapore Biennale 2022, a selected participant for da:ns LAB 2023: IDIOSYNCRASIES at Esplanade – Theatres on the Bay, and a resident writer for Centre 42’s New Scripts Residency 2021.

From 2020-2024, she was the Co-Founder & Company Lead of Feelers, a research lab of artists and designers focused on the intersections of art and technology.

She has a BA(Hons) in English Literature and Creative Writing from the University of Warwick.
