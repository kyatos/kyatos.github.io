now




I'm in a period of self-inquiry after emerging from over a decade of self-finding and trauma recovery. Feeling surprised by the sharp clarity and wisdom within reality which I've missed for so long because of the psychological landscapes I had to traverse, and the specific tools & narratives I used to make that journey. Slowly accepting that the reverbrations of that time will continue to be felt, even as I close many chapters and see healing take on very tangible forms.

As I shed, I'm focused on building capacities I suspect I need for the work I want to do in the future. I'm taking classes in various areas of interest, like Odissi dance and Korean language. I'm working hard on slow species, my Substack newsletter, as well as poems for my second book. I'm figuring out how to organise my life around healthy boundaries.

I'm grounded in two questions Maggie Rogers posed herself, namely:

How do I keep the things sacred in my life really safe?
How do I reconstruct every aspect of my life and my career to keep thosse things at the centre?

sacred things

  • my ability to think clearly and feel deeply
  • the unknown territories of my thinking
  • my practice of writing, my pleasure in writing
  • my most exciting and resonant questions
  • solitude to develop my island of thinking w/ minimal external influence & interference
  • my ability to pay attention to the things that matter
  • my peace, wisdom, and intuition

things I'm saying yes to

  • meeting new people, especially people outside my usual circles and comfort zone
  • more time alone, more time to follow my own thinking to its ends
  • gathering information about my mental patterns & needs to build routines that free up mental space & energy
  • freelance projects that give me artistic joy. bonus points if they also nurture my weaker skills (public speaking, hosting strangers, etc.)
  • working with people who value the specific kinds of work and perspectives I bring
  • finding independent thinkers whose principles and processes I can learn from

things I'm saying no to

  • projects and commitments where I'm in charge of other people
  • independent projects where I'm the one in charge, especially when it includes grant writing and producing
  • last minute commitments. extra points deducted for: being mostly used as filler, being paid badly, lack of communication
