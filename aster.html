flora - hiroshi yoshimura

Who is setting out on this journey? I have become unrecognizable to myself, but tethered by a steady awareness of this moment. And this one. I am not afraid of how long it will take, and how quickly it will be over. I rest close to the body of time; we breathe together. When I sleep I dream as though the world has no edges, and I move through my wakeful hours with a sense of endlessness. Submitting to time, I have become freed of its torment. I am pleased and amused and pissed off, and every feeling passes like a wave.

These scales, sting, gills, wings, feathers, sepal, beak, mane... they complete my manufactured image, this machinic spirit. The wheels of fortune turn me like a finely-toothed gear.

Leaving this place, I shed this iteration of the world and myself the way a snake gives up its old skin. Softened, more vulnerable, and less bound by the previous membrane, my body alters in shape ever so slightly. In this way, I maintain a slow disappearance -- it is my constant state.
